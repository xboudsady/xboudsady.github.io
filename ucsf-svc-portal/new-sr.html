Event Set-Up Work Orders


Use this request for custodial set-ups to support events and functions.

Set-ups for events and functions are funded by the customer, with the exception of RCO sponsored events.


Setups for Parnassus Campus:

Please book 5 days in advance to ensure setup can be scheduled.

Include the following information in the work description:

  • Event Date(s)
  • Event Time (period during which booking is actually taking place)
  • Reserved Time (total time for which the room is reserved; generally event time plus setup/teardown hours)
  • Required Features:
    • easels
    • recycling bins
    • extra trash cans
    • extra chairs and tables
  • Include setup style and for special setups include diagram.

Questions?

Please contact the Customer Service Center at 476-2021, or email us at fmcustservice@fm.ucsf.edu.

Facilities Funded Work

Use this request for regular building maintenance and repair work such as restroom maintenance and burned out light bulbs.

Facilities funded work is generally for maintenance of...

  • building corridors
  • building systems such as ventilation
  • lobbies
  • restrooms
  • classrooms
  • and other public spaces
While there are exceptions, generally work needed within an office,or lab, or suite will be CUSTOMER FUNDED WORK and will need a DPA/Fund for recharge.

Following is a partial list of both maintenance services funded by the state and maintenance services that are not state funded and therefore chargeable to the customer. These are examples and this list is not comprehensive.

Call the Customer Service Center at 476-2021 for help in confirming funding source.

Projects Work Order

Whereas other types of Work Orders are for quick and direct simple-to-remedy scopes of work, the Projects tab is for scopesthat would involve general contractors, or design consultants.

This might be as simple as installing a door ina corridor or adding a small office within an existing suite.

It might be as complex as a new stem cell research building on the edge of a cliff. If it is work that requires more than a single skilled tradesperson, start here.


Examples:

  • Work to be Capitalized
  • Phased Work
  • Renovations that require multiple trades
  • Structural Engineering / Geotechnical Engineering
  • Remodels/ Restorations / Consults
  • Elevator Projects
  • ROM estimates (where design is involved)
  • Work involving corridor penetrations
  • Egress related work
  • DM Projects
  • SEP Projects
  • Major Roofing - with logistics
  • Studies
  • New Buildings
  • New Labs
  • Building Infrastructure replacement/ Improvement
  • Work in Public Areas (lobbies, stairwells, entries)
