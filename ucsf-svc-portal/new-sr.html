Facilities Service Request

Generally work needed within an office,or lab, or suite will be customer funded work and will require a chart of accounts string or speedtype for recharge.

Following is a partial list of maintenance services funded by the state and services that are not state funded and therefore chargeable to the customer. These are examples and this list is not comprehensive.

If you need assistance to determine if your request is billable, please call Customer Service Center at 415-476-2021.


* Only applies to state-supported spaces. Does not apply to leased buildings.


     Custodial Services

Facilities Funded Services

  • Carpetcleaning, floor strip and wax done every 24 months in public spaces
  • Graffiti removal in public spaces
  • Routine* cleaning services performed daily in general use spaces such as lobbies, corridors, restrooms, and other public areas
  • Routine* cleaning services in labs, offices, classrooms, and other supported areas
  • Spill cleanup in public areas
  • Trash removal (regular trash in standard containers)
  • Recycling and compost removal
  • Routine scheduled pest control
  • Exterior window washing provided every 5 years, as budget allows
  • * Routine services include emptying trash, sweeping and/ or mopping, and vacuuming. See the Custodial Frequency Chart (page 20) for further details.

Rechargeable Services

  • Carpet cleaning, floor strip and wax in all department-occupied, non-public spaces
  • Event setups
  • High-clean requests
  • Spill clean-up in department spaces
  • Project clean-up over and above routine cleaning
  • Special requests such as additional trash cans, walk-off mats, special cleanup above and beyond established routine cleaning
  • Trash removal of irregular items or excessive accumulations above and beyond everyday norms
  • Interior window washing
  • Exterior window washing beyond the building’s established scheduled maintenance

Projects Work Order

Whereas other types of Work Orders are for quick and direct simple-to-remedy scopes of work, the Projects tab is for scopesthat would involve general contractors, or design consultants.

This might be as simple as installing a door ina corridor or adding a small office within an existing suite.

It might be as complex as a new stem cell research building on the edge of a cliff. If it is work that requires more than a single skilled tradesperson, start here.


Examples:

  • Work to be Capitalized
  • Phased Work
  • Renovations that require multiple trades
  • Structural Engineering / Geotechnical Engineering
  • Remodels/ Restorations / Consults
  • Elevator Projects
  • ROM estimates (where design is involved)
  • Work involving corridor penetrations
  • Egress related work
  • DM Projects
  • SEP Projects
  • Major Roofing - with logistics
  • Studies
  • New Buildings
  • New Labs
  • Building Infrastructure replacement/ Improvement
  • Work in Public Areas (lobbies, stairwells, entries)

Signs Order

You are here. Having the proper signage on all UCSF property is essential for clarity of communication. Whether you need to update an old sign or create a new one, we can help with the entire process.

These services are provided on customer recharge basis. UCSF has signage standards to ensure consistency and compliance with ADA Guidelines.


How to order a signage or nameplate?

  1. Submit a work order online and reference the facilities work order number (E.g. C12345)
  2. Fill out the form below with the details of the sign(s) and/or nameplate(s) you are requesting, and a coordinator will contact you shortly to confirm.
